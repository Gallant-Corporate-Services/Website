Manufacturing and sustainability, under one roof.

Two operating divisions give Gallant reach most agencies lack — a manufacturing arm for our own branding, apparel and signage, and a circular economy division that turns used event materials into new, sustainable products.

GBI — The in-house manufacturing advantage

What is designed in the boardroom is exactly what is delivered on site.

Outsourcing critical branding adds risk to cost, quality and timelines. GBI removes it by keeping manufacturing in-house. With over a decade of experience, GBI produces industrial apparel, large-format signage and corporate branding — on time and on budget.

For procurement officers and brand managers, that means fewer points of failure and one party accountable for everything seen by delegates and the public.

GLA — Circular economy, built into every event

Every event leaves materials behind. We put them back to work.

Events are resource-heavy. Gallant Loop Africa is our circular economy division, built to change that. GLA upcycles post-use materials — banners, lanyards, stage fabrics and signage — into new delegate kits and corporate products, cutting waste and giving clients eco-conscious merchandise.

For clients and donors with ESG targets, GLA turns disposal cost into a measurable, reportable advantage.
